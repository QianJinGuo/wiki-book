#  [Everything we announced at Sessions 2026](/blog/everything-we-announced-at-sessions-2026)
[April 29, 2026](/blog/everything-we-announced-at-sessions-2026)
[ ](https://twitter.com/gaybrick) [Will Gaybrick](https://twitter.com/gaybrick) Product and Business
This morning at our annual conference, Stripe Sessions, we shared 288 new products and features with more than 9,000 business leaders and builders. We’re making Stripe even more programmable; protecting and propelling your business with the strength of the Stripe network; and building economic infrastructure for AI. 
Here’s everything we announced.
## Payments
Today, we launched and demoed payments infrastructure for the next era of commerce. We expanded the Agentic Commerce Suite through partnerships with Meta and Google; announced a new way to grant agents the ability to pay with Link’s agent wallet; and unveiled Checkout studio, which helps you build, analyze, and optimize high-converting checkouts in a few clicks. Here’s what’s new:
**Agentic commerce**
  * You can now sell through AI agents by uploading your product catalog and managing agent access directly from the Stripe Dashboard with the [Agentic Commerce Suite](https://docs.stripe.com/agentic-commerce).
  * We previewed the ability for [platforms to use the Agentic Commerce Suite](https://docs.stripe.com/connect/saas/tasks/enable-in-context-selling-on-ai-agents), allowing their connected accounts to become agent-ready with discovery, checkout, payments, and fraud detection handled through a single Stripe integration. 
  * We partnered with Meta to [enable native checkout inside ads on Facebook](https://stripe.com/newsroom/news/checkout-for-facebook) so discovery and purchase happen in one flow.
  * Customers will soon be able to buy your products in AI Mode and the Gemini app via the Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP), through a new partnership between Stripe and Google.
  * Agents can now programmatically transact with your business via microtransactions, recurring payments, and more with the[ Machine Payments Protocol](https://mpp.dev/) (MPP), co-authored by Stripe and Tempo.
  * You can now [accept payments from agents over MPP](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/machine/mpp) in stablecoins as well as fiat through cards, Klarna, and Affirm via [Shared Payment Tokens](https://docs.stripe.com/agentic-commerce/concepts/shared-payment-tokens) (SPTs), using our Payment Intents API. 
**Link**
  * You can now grant agents the ability to pay with [Link’s agent wallet](https://link.com/agents), while maintaining control via spending approvals and full purchase visibility.
  * Link now supports additional payment methods. Via Link, US businesses can now accept [Pix](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/link/pix) in Brazil and [stablecoins](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/link/stablecoins). We also previewed the ability for US businesses to accept [UPI](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/link/upi) via Link in India.
  * You can now see Link’s impact on your conversion, authorization rates, and payments costs in a [dedicated tab in the Dashboard](https://dashboard.stripe.com/link). 
**Optimized Checkout Suite**
  * We previewed [Checkout studio](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/checkout-studio), a new way for you to configure, analyze, and optimize your checkout with an AI assistant, live transaction replay, A/B testing, and personalized recommendations. 
  * We previewed Stripe Checkout’s new [embedded form](https://docs.stripe.com/checkout/form/quickstart), built for embedded use cases like sidebars, chat boxes, and modals. 
  * More payment methods, including [Pix](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/pix) and [UPI](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/upi), now support subscriptions, localized currency presentment, and cross-border payments.
  * You can now accept [Sunbit](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/sunbit) in the US, [Bizum](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/bizum) in Spain, and [Pay by Bank](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/pay-by-bank) in Finland.
  * You can now accept [Bizum](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/bizum), [BLIK](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/blik), and [Pay by Bank](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/pay-by-bank) for cross-border payments. 
  * You can now accept [TWINT](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/twint) for recurring transactions. 
  * You can now automatically detect and display your customer’s preferred currency with a new [Adaptive Pricing](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/currencies/localize-prices/adaptive-pricing) AI model that analyzes dozens of session signals in real time. 
  * You can now use [Adaptive Pricing](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/currencies/localize-prices/adaptive-pricing) to localize subscription prices to your customer’s domestic currency. 
  * We previewed a new way for your customers to [save a card in your app by tapping it](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/mobile/embedded-tap-to-add) on their Android device instead of manually entering the card information. 
**Stripe Terminal**
  * We announced the [Stripe Reader T600](https://stripe.com/terminal/t600), a new countertop device with an eight-inch screen and the power to run custom apps that support experiences like loyalty and upsells.
  * We announced the ability to process in-person payments with Terminal in [15 additional markets](https://docs.stripe.com/terminal/payments/collect-card-payment/supported-card-brands), including Hong Kong and Mexico.
  * We announced the ability to accept [additional payment methods](https://docs.stripe.com/terminal/payments/collect-card-payment/supported-card-brands#payment-method-availability) such as Alipay, Klarna, and UnionPay International for in-person payments with Terminal. 
  * We previewed Terminal’s [standalone mode](https://docs.stripe.com/terminal/payments/standalone-mode#set-up-standalone-mode), so you can quickly start accepting payments on Stripe readers with no code or point of sale required. 
**Stripe Managed Payments**
  * All digital businesses can now use****[Managed Payments](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/managed-payments), Stripe’s merchant of record solution. It helps digital businesses sell globally by handling indirect tax compliance in 80+ countries, as well as fraud prevention, dispute management, and customer support. 
**Payments Intelligence Suite**
  * We previewed the ability to [A/B test Authorization Boost](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/analytics/optimization/a-b-testing) against your existing payments performance. 
  * You can now use Authorization Boost’s new AI-powered optimizations, including support for [Data Only authentication flows](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/3d-secure/strong-customer-authentication-exemptions#data-only) and [PINless debit retries](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/analytics/optimization#adaptive-acceptance), helping increase acceptance rates by an average of 3.8% and lower processing costs by up to 3.3%. 
  * You can now use Stripe’s 3DS solution on a [standalone basis](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/3d-secure/standalone-three-d-secure)—enabling regulatory compliance, including support for exemptions, and fraud protection to payments processed by another payment service provider. 
  * You can now use the [Stripe Dashboard assistant](https://docs.stripe.com/assistant) for payments analytics, which investigates performance issues, diagnoses root causes, and recommends next steps in natural language. 
## Radar
Millions of global businesses use Stripe Radar to fight fraud, from leading AI companies like OpenAI, Anthropic, ElevenLabs, and Cursor to global enterprises like PepsiCo and Hertz. Today, we announced the biggest-ever upgrades to Radar, helping you to defend against new types of fraud—including token abuse and account fraud—protect more of your payments on and off Stripe, and win more disputes. Here’s what’s new: 
  * You can now identify high-risk trials without blocking legitimate customers using [Radar’s free trial abuse prevention](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/free-trial-abuse). 
  * We previewed [Radar’s bot abuse prevention](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/bot-abuse), designed to accurately distinguish legitimate AI agents from fraudulent actors. 
  * You can now use a new payment signal to identify [fraudulent payments](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/multiprocessor#fraudulent-payment) on and off Stripe with [Stripe Signals](https://docs.stripe.com/signals). 
  * We previewed additional payment signals to [predict disputes](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/multiprocessor#fraudulent-dispute) and [early fraud warnings](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/multiprocessor#early-fraud-warning), and detect [pay-as-you-go abuse](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/pay-as-you-go-abuse) before invoices go unpaid with [Signals](https://docs.stripe.com/signals). 
  * We previewed new customer signals to detect [multi-account abuse](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/multi-account-and-account-sharing-abuse#multi-account-abuse) and [account sharing abuse](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/multi-account-and-account-sharing-abuse#account-sharing-abuse) with [Signals](https://docs.stripe.com/signals), helping you distinguish real customers from bad actors before they cost you money. 
  * We previewed new merchant signals to assess [fraud risk for new accounts](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/fraudulent-merchant), predict [merchant delinquency](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/merchant-delinquency-risk), and analyze [websites for suspicious activity](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/fraudulent-website) using LLM-powered reviews with [Signals](https://docs.stripe.com/signals). 
  * We previewed new Stripe Issuing authorization signals that predict the likelihood that a [card authorization is fraudulent](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/issuing-authorization-evaluation), extending protection to non-Stripe issuers, banks, and fintechs. 
  * You can now use Radar’s fraud protection to block high-risk transactions on [all supported payment methods](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/local-payment-methods), including bank debits; wallets; buy now, pay later (BNPL) options; and stablecoins. 
  * We announced [custom Radar models](https://docs.stripe.com/radar/custom-fraud-models) that are trained on your business signals along with Stripe’s global network intelligence for more accurate fraud detection.
  * You can now access [upgraded fraud intervention models for Checkout](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/checkout/how-checkout-works?payment-ui=stripe-hosted#manage-risk) that enable precise, low-friction interventions, such as targeted CAPTCHAs, to reduce fraud while improving conversion. 
  * You can now receive [AI-powered recommendations](https://docs.stripe.com/disputes/set-up-smart-disputes#provide-more-data-at-dispute-time) for additional evidence fields, such as tracking number or customer usage logs, to supplement Smart Disputes evidence.
  * You can now store persistent documentation, like terms and conditions, in your evidence library. [Smart Disputes](https://docs.stripe.com/disputes/set-up-smart-disputes) can pull from this library automatically, so you don’t have to submit the same materials repeatedly. 
## Revenue
Agentic activity requires new, real-time infrastructure for metering, rating, alerting, and payments. We upgraded our Revenue suite to support new AI-native business models with dimensional pricing and streaming payments. We also made Stripe more programmable with three new Stripe Billing customizations and expanded access to real-time, ready-to-query data. Here’s what’s new: 
**Stripe Billing**
  * You can now power a broader range of [usage-based and hybrid pricing models](https://docs.stripe.com/billing/subscriptions/usage-based) with Metronome, a Stripe product. You can manage commits, multidimensional pricing structures, bespoke contracts, and more, with real-time revenue visibility down to the account, contract, and product level. 
  * You can now access and manage Metronome contracts, customers, and data directly in the Stripe Dashboard with the [Metronome app](https://docs.metronome.com/guides/get-started/stripe-marketplace-app).
  * We announced the ability to support streaming payments by combining [Metronome](https://docs.metronome.com/guides/customers-billing/set-up-notifications/streaming-alerts) and [Tempo](https://docs.tempo.xyz/guide/machine-payments/streamed-payments), allowing you to get paid the instant value is delivered and cost incurred. 
  * You can now [track credit balances](https://docs.stripe.com/billing/usage-based) at industry-leading rates, [get instant low-balance alerts](https://docs.stripe.com/billing/usage-based), and [configure automatic credit top-ups](https://docs.stripe.com/billing/usage-based). 
  * We previewed support for [three new types of Billing customizations](https://docs.stripe.com/billing/scripts), allowing you to programmatically define how subscription items appear on invoices, how prorations behave, and how balances and credits are applied. 
  * We previewed [payment plans](https://docs.stripe.com/invoicing/payment-plans) that you can use to accept installment payments with automated collection, giving your customers more flexibility and improving cash flow. 
  * We previewed [subscription invoice revisions](https://docs.stripe.com/invoicing/invoice-edits), so you can edit finalized subscription invoices and automatically void the original invoice. 
**Stripe Tax**
  * You can now [automate US tax filing](https://docs.stripe.com/tax/file-with-taxjar), track status, and view filing details in the Stripe Dashboard, powered by TaxJar. 
  * We previewed additional Stripe Tax Connectors for [Shopify](https://docs.stripe.com/use-stripe-apps/shopify) and [NetSuite](https://marketplace.stripe.com/apps/paypack), so you can extend Tax to platforms where you already do business. 
  * We previewed the ability for Tax to [verify tax IDs](https://docs.stripe.com/payments/advanced/tax?api-integration=checkout#real-time-tax-id-validation) against government databases during checkout flows, blocking invalid IDs. 
  * Payment Intents API users can now calculate and record tax on a payment [with a single parameter](https://docs.stripe.com/tax/payment-intent)—no separate API calls required. 
  * Businesses located in Liechtenstein and Mexico and digital sellers in Sri Lanka can now [use Tax](https://docs.stripe.com/tax/supported-countries). We will also offer full support for businesses in Malaysia and remote sellers in French and Spanish territories soon. 
**Data**
  * We previewed the ability to retrieve real-time subscription metrics within AI agents using the [Stripe MCP](https://docs.stripe.com/mcp), or within [Stripe Console](https://docs.stripe.com/dashboard/console). 
  * We previewed [Stripe Database](https://docs.stripe.com/stripe-data/stripe-database), giving you real-time Stripe data in a managed, hosted, read-only Postgres database. You can spin up the database with one click from the Dashboard or a single CLI command, allowing you to build and ship applications faster.
  * We previewed the [next generation of Stripe Data Pipeline](https://docs.stripe.com/stripe-data/data-pipeline-next-gen), enabling real-time data syncs to Google Sheets with more data destinations coming soon. 
  * You can now sync all of your Stripe data directly to [Databricks](https://docs.stripe.com/stripe-data/access-data-in-warehouse/data-warehouses) with Data Pipeline. 
  * We previewed the ability for you to programmatically retrieve prebuilt financial reports across accounts in your organization using the [Reports API v2](https://docs.stripe.com/reports/v2-api). 
  * We previewed the ability for businesses using Stripe Sigma to execute custom SQL queries programmatically for advanced data modeling and reporting with the [Reports API v2](https://docs.stripe.com/reports/query-runs). 
## Money Management
The AI economy is growing at breakneck speed, and more companies have a global footprint from day one. But the tools to manage your money haven’t kept up, with finances typically scattered across half a dozen apps and spreadsheets. We previewed new capabilities to help you store, spend, and manage money—including a Stripe card that rewards you for spending, free transfers to other Stripe Treasury users, and MCP support for Treasury APIs so you can build your own financial agents. Here’s what’s new:
