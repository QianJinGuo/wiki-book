# Amazon launches Supply Chain Services for businesses of all sizes
## Introducing Amazon Supply Chain Services: Amazon's logistics network, now open to every business
Any business can now move, store, and deliver everything from raw materials to finished products using the same supply chain that supports Amazon and its independent selling partners.
## Key takeaways
- Over the past three years, hundreds of thousands of Amazon sellers have trusted the company's logistics network to move, store, and deliver hundreds of millions of packages across third-party facilities, warehouses, and sales channels beyond the Amazon store.
- Today, Amazon is launching Amazon Supply Chain Services (ASCS), opening its freight, warehousing, and delivery capabilities to any business regardless of size or sales channels.
- ASCS brings together decades of Amazon's supply chain expertise into an integrated offering that businesses can use end-to-end or pick and choose individual services based on their needs.
## How it works
Amazon Supply Chain Services allows businesses to:
1. **Move** — Use Amazon's global freight network to ship raw materials and finished goods across continents
2. **Store** — Access Amazon's warehouse infrastructure and third-party logistics partner network
3. **Deliver** — Leverage Amazon's last-mile delivery capabilities to reach end customers
Businesses can use the full suite or individual services based on their supply chain needs.
## Why it matters
Amazon's supply chain has been one of its core competitive advantages. By opening this to other businesses, Amazon is positioning itself as a logistics provider competing with traditional 3PL companies like DHL, FedEx, and UPS.
