##  data-block-key=ori9n>Improving AI infrastructure
<p data-block-key=4hkn5>AlphaEvolve has graduated from pilot testing to becoming a core component of our infrastructure. AlphaEvolve has been used as a regular tool to optimize the design of the next generation of <a href="https://cloud.google.com/tpu?e=48754805" rel=noopener target=_blank>TPUs</a>. It also helped discover more efficient <a href=https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22425 rel=noopener target=_blank>cache replacement policies</a>, achieving in two days what previously required a concerted, human-intensive effort spanning months.
<p data-block-key=7re9u>“<em>AlphaEvolve began optimizing the lowest levels of hardware powering our AI stacks. It proposed a circuit design so counterintuitive yet efficient that it was integrated directly into the silicon of our next-generation TPUs. This is the latest example of TPU brains helping design next-generation TPU bodies.</em>” — Jeff Dean, Chief Scientist, Google DeepMind and Google Research
<p data-block-key=1og3e>AlphaEvolve improved the efficiency of <a href=https://cloud.google.com/spanner rel=noopener target=_blank>Google Spanner</a> by refining its <a href=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Log-structured_merge-tree rel=noopener target=_blank>Log-Structured Merge-tree</a> compaction heuristics. This optimization reduced 'write amplification'—the ratio of data written to storage versus the original request—by 20%. It also provided insights for <a href=https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21096 rel=noopener target=_blank>new compiler optimization strategies</a> that reduced the storage footprint of software by nearly 9%.
